Taj Mahal

One of the most famous historical sites in India, the Taj Mahal of Agra dates back to the 17th century. It was built by Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor, in the memory of Mumtaz Mahal, his third wife. It is also the abode of the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

Qutub Minar, Delhi

Delhi’s Qutub Minar ranks amongst the top 10 historical places in India. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Qutub Minar, also known as the Victory Tower, is the tallest brick minaret in the whole world, standing tall at a height of 72.5 metres.

Fatehpur Sikri, Uttar Pradesh

An architectural gem and one of the most exquisite historical places in India is Fatehpur Sikri, a town in the Agra district of Uttar Pradesh. A recognized UNESCO World Heritage Site, Fatehpur Sikri is made predominantly out of red sandstone, and dates back to the late 16th century, when it was founded by Mughal Emperor Akbar.

Victoria Memorial

Ranking amongst the top 10 historical places in India, Kolkata’s Victoria Memorial was constructed in honour of Queen Victoria, making it one of the relics of the British Raj. Built entirely out of white marble, the Victoria Memorial is almost a replica of its namesake in London.

Humayun’s Tomb, Delhi

Amongst the oldest historical places in India, Humayun’s Tomb in Delhi, along the banks of the Yamuna River, was also the first garden-tomb in the country. Built by Emperor Humayun’s wife in memory of her husband in the 16th century, this monument is surrounded by double-storied gateways and four gardens, and looks simply phenomenal.
